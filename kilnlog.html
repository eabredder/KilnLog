Temperature depression (wet-bulb wick health)

A healthy wick typically shows 4–8 °C depression. Values include a small upward offset from the dry-bulb heater. Watch for the trend collapsing toward zero — that traditionally signals the wick needs re-wetting, regardless of the baseline.

Kiln vs. Outside Temperature

Yellow shading = kiln warmer than outside (solar gain). Blue shading = kiln cooler than outside. Dry-bulb reads slightly high (+0.5–2 °C) due to the sensor's anti-condensation heater — trends are reliable, absolute values include a small upward bias.
